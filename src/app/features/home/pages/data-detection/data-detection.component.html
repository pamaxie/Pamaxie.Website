How we determine data types

Since it’s hard for service providers to determine data types, we offer service endpoints to do it for them as well. This allows for in flight analysis without having to rely on data extensions. The way we determine the type of the data provided to us is via something called “magic numbers”. The magic numbers of file-types are essentially pieces of data that make files distinguishable.
Once we determined the magic numbers, we then put it in the processing queue for the appropriate data type. Which leads us to our next section onto what kind of data types we intend to support for now and which ones we intend to support in the future.

Supported data types now and in the future

In the initial release of Pamaxie we will primarily focus on determining the data type of images. This is to focus on ensuring our scanning system works reliably and well.
Our secondary goal from here on out would be detecting things like Videos. This is because it can build on top of our image detection and can work over a key-frame extraction system using something like FFMPEG.
Our tertiary goal would be developing detection systems and determinations for texts. We do not plan to detect hate-speech or anything like this. We believe dedicated solutions are better for that. We plan to detect potentially triggering messages (like messages talking about self harm or other topics that can make people uncomfortable).

Our quaternary goal will probably be working on detecting harmful files (like executables or zip files). This will take a longer while since we need to start building up specific research projects into researching Harmful files. We will not be able to replace your Anti-Virus software, but we do intend to detect the most harmful type’s of files that are well known to be harmful to users.
