Building an accepting space

Our open source philosophy is build around creating an accepting space. This means no matter how much experience you have, no matter who you work for you are welcome. We also enforce strict policies against discriminating against racial or sexual minorities.
We believe everyone can contribute someone important to the project but we can only achieve that by offering an accepting and welcoming space to people no matter their background, history or origin.
All of us have to follow this code of conduct and have to agree to it. It does not matter if you’re just using our API or contributing to it.

Offering help to those who need it

Our communities are build to help each other out and help people grow their skills along the way. We believe we all have to start somewhere, so offering help and staying friendly with inexperienced people is important to us.
This also means we’re happy to help people who aren’t experienced with our code-base by giving introductions to it and explaining how most things work.

This also means we’re happy to help people inexperienced with programming if we implement more complex solutions in our project and explain those solutions to them.

We all are equal

The last important point is that we are all equal. No matter if you’re part of our internal development team or an external developer. We are even happy to work together with other researchers and are willing to share all of our datasets to those who can prove they need it.

We believe data and information needs to be shared to not get lost and that everyone helping has their place in our team, no matter if long time contributor with thousands of lines contributed or new fresh developer without any experience in our project.

Build on Open Solutions

We try to build all of our APIs services on open source or at least openly accessible solutions. This ensures that even if someone wants to use other service providers with many of the services we use they are able to.
Obviously this isn’t always possible but if you think you found something we could make more accessible to everyone please let us know.
We are for inclusion no matter if you use AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, host servers yourself or just want to use docker containers on your own machine.

Some final words

If this spiked your interest and you want to check out or help with our mission, feel free to scroll down to the bottom of the page and look at the resources for community interaction we offer there. We are always happy to have new people who want to help us with this important mission!
